Project Goals

Reduce textbook costs Provide free, high-quality digital resources
Integrate modern technology AI tools, advanced calculators, R software
Develop educational materials Guided lecture notes, interactive question banks
Foster inclusive learning Accessible materials encouraging real-world connections
Sustain faculty development Training, peer review, continuous improvement

Project Objectives by Course

MAT104 Team • Dr. Dora & Dr. Angela Cornelius

Objective Details
Create OER Version Develop MAT104 on MyOpenMath platform for preservice teachers
Develop Diagnostic Test Assess students' foundational math skills
Create Pedagogical Assessments Quizzes, assignments, and tests rich in pedagogy
Produce Support Materials Modular resources to address common learning gaps

MAT120 Team • Drs. Mangala Kothari & Prabha Betne

Objective Details
Integrate Technology R software, advanced calculators (TI-83/TI-84)
Revise Course Materials Class notes, PowerPoints, worksheets, homework
Create Tech-Friendly Assignments Real-world data applications with technology focus
Assess Learning Outcomes Compare final exam scores between pilot and control sections

Calculus Sequence Team • Drs. Tao Chen & Bukurie Gjoci

Objective Details
Create Calculus III Template Master section on MyOpenMath with full resources
Develop Calculus I Lecture Notes Custom materials for LaGuardia students
Include Multimedia Resources Reading materials, lecture videos, homework, quizzes
Enhance Reading Skills Improve students' ability to read mathematical materials

MAT231 Team • Drs. Marina Dedlovskaya & Shenglan Yuan

Objective Details
Develop Comprehensive Workbook 12 reading assignments, worksheets, worked examples
Create Study Guides Guided problem sets and interactive activities
Expand Question Bank Focus on difficult topics (logic, combinatorics, etc.)
Pilot Materials Implement in at least two sections of MAT231 and MAT210

Project Requirements

CUNY OER Requirements

Course Designation

  • Mark as "Zero Textbook Cost" in CUNYfirst
  • Update course attribute for all OER sections

Licensing

  • Use Creative Commons licenses
  • Allow reuse, remixing, redistribution
  • Ensure all components are openly licensed

Accessibility

  • Meet CUNY accessibility standards
  • Ensure materials usable by all students
  • Follow web accessibility guidelines

Reporting

  • Submit mid-year report (mid-January)
  • Submit year-end report (May 1)
  • Include assessment data if collected

Attribution

  • Label as "Funded by CUNY OER Initiative"
  • Include proper attribution on all materials

Publication

  • Publish within 18 months of funding
  • Upload to CUNY platforms
  • Make publicly available

Course Conversion LOI

When to Use Course Conversion LOI

Converting existing courses To Zero Textbook Cost (ZTC) format
Using OER materials Open Educational Resources
Library licensed materials Resources freely available to CUNY students
Freely available internet resources Materials available at no cost online

Faculty Commitments for Course Conversion

Requirement Details
Progress Meetings Attend three meetings: beginning, mid-year, and end-year
Written Updates Provide updates for mid-year and end-year reports
Pre-conversion Syllabus Submit to campus OER representative (not shared publicly)
Teach Converted Course In next semester assigned after grant year ends
Syllabus Labeling Mark as ZTC, OER, or Zero Textbook Course
CUNYFirst Designation Communicate course should be marked ZTC in CUNYFirst
Post-conversion Syllabus Provide to campus OER representative for records
Public Sharing Share at least one deliverable publicly
Open Licensing Use Creative Commons licenses for original materials
Material Upload Upload to Academic Works or opened.cuny.edu
Accessibility Ensure all materials meet CUNY accessibility standards

Content Creation LOI

When to Use Content Creation LOI

Creating new educational materials From scratch rather than adapting existing content
Openly licensed materials For reuse, remixing, and redistribution
New OER development Rather than course conversion
